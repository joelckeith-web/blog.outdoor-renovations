## Critical Damage Assessment Checklist

Walk your property now, before Sunday's storms arrive. Here's what Circle C Ranch homeowners need to inspect immediately:

**Foundation and Drainage Systems**
Check all foundation areas for standing water or moisture intrusion. Circle C Ranch's elevation changes can create unexpected water flow patterns during heavy rain events. Look for new erosion channels along driveways and walkways - these indicate your surface drainage isn't handling the volume.

Inspect gutters and downspouts for clogs from the recent winds. A single clogged downspout can dump hundreds of gallons directly against your foundation during the next storm. Clear all debris now.

**Trees and Large Plants**
Look for tilted trees, especially younger specimens planted in the last 2-3 years. Saturated clay soil loses its holding power, and even healthy root systems can fail. Check for broken or hanging branches that could fall during Sunday's storms.

Native Texas plants like live oaks and cedar elms typically weather these conditions well, but stressed non-native species may show leaf drop or branch damage from the wind stress.

**Hardscape Elements**
Walk all patios, walkways, and retaining walls. Clay soil expansion and contraction can shift pavers and create trip hazards. Look for any stones or decorative elements that have moved - they'll move further during the next storm.

Check fence lines for loose boards or tilted posts. The 39 mph gusts may have loosened hardware that seemed secure before.

## Emergency Preparation Steps

**Immediate Drainage Improvements**
You can't install major [drainage systems](https://outdoorreno.com/services/irrigation-drainage/circle-c-ranch) before Sunday, but you can make critical adjustments. Extend downspouts away from foundations using temporary extensions if permanent ones are damaged.

Create temporary berms using soil or mulch to direct water away from vulnerable areas. Focus on protecting basements, crawl spaces, and low-lying patio areas.

Clear all surface drainage paths of leaves, branches, and debris. Even small obstructions can cause major backups during heavy rain.

**Irrigation System Adjustments**
Turn off automatic irrigation systems immediately. The recent 0.86 inches of rainfall provides adequate soil moisture for at least 5-7 days in Circle C Ranch's clay soils. [Smart irrigation controllers](https://www.epa.gov/watersense/smart-irrigation-controllers) would automatically adjust for recent precipitation, potentially saving 30-50% on water usage while protecting plant health.

Kyle Stoutenger recommends: "After significant rainfall events, I tell clients to manually override their irrigation for at least a week. Clay soils stay saturated much longer than homeowners realize, and adding more water to already stressed root systems often causes more damage than drought stress would."

**Securing Outdoor Elements**
Remove or secure all loose patio furniture, decorative pots, and garden tools. Items that survived 39 mph winds may not survive stronger gusts if they're already loosened.

Check all [outdoor structures](https://outdoorreno.com/services/custom-carpentry/circle-c-ranch) for loose connections. Pergolas, gazebos, and shade structures take tremendous stress during wind events. Tighten all bolts and hardware connections now.

> **Key Takeaway**
